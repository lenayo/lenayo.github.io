MERRIAM, Kansas -- It doesn't take pearls and diamonds to dress up an outfit anymore. Casual clothing is taking on a classier design of its own. Today's busy woman strives to achieve a polished look and the Lee Apparel has created a collection that captures her appeal for sophistication, with City Elements, a new collection from Riveted by Lee.

City Elements brings class and chic together, without sacrificing comfort and price. This collection takes advantage of blended fabrications as well as constructed waistbands, extra buttons, hidden zippers, washable fabrics and a variety of inseam options to meet consumer needs. City Elements' tailored bottoms and classic tops address the latest trend of dressing up casual looks.

"Fashion is without-a-doubt back," said Betsy Pike, trend analyst for Lee. "Once the 'bad word' of the nineties, fashion has returned to the scene with sleek lines, feminine curves and a cleaner look."

This trend in the fashion industry responds to the contemporary woman looking for an elegant, tailored style with innovative designs at a modest price. City Elements offers the first and only upscale look to casual dressing in the moderate retail tier.

"Lee is a lifestyle brand, and City Elements offers our consumer the more polished look she wants without sacrificing the flexibility and affordability she's come to expect from Lee," said Kathy Collins, vice president of marketing for Lee.

Adding classic pieces to an already hip outfit can enhance any wardrobe. City Elements launches in Fall 2001 and features four pants, one skirt and three tops. The pieces retail at around $44.
