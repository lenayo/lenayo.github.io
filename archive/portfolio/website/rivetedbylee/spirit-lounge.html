Widely recognized as Abby Lockhart on NBC's ER, Maura Tierney has traded in her scrubs for Riveted's Low Rise for our new ad campaign. As Tierney knows, this ad campaign is not just about looking good, it's about feeling good. For every pair of low-rise jeans purchased during June, Lee will donate $1 to longtime partner, the Susan G. Komen Breast Cancer Foundation (up to $50,000). Between shots, here are some snippets of what Maura had to say about her life when she isn't working:

On looking so innocent: "I'm not exactly a Hollywood wild child, but I have had a brush or two with the law. I am a reformed jaywalker. There was a time when I collected jaywalking tickets like they were stamps. And, as it turns out, you're supposed to pay those. Let's just say I came dangerously close to spending a night in the slammer."

On trasitioning from New York to L.A.: "I miss the subways. I miss all the interaction. Even if you're not talking to people, you know what they've done all day just by what they're wearing and the things they're carrying. In L.A., people are always a car's length away. There's a lot more human contact in New York... even it fit's even if it's not always pretty!"

On her pug, the royal Rose Kennedy: "My friends always laugh that Rose Kennedy is my hobby. But if you think I pamper her, you should see my husband. It's to the point where he won't let us go on vacation without her. We've even driven cross-country with Rose Kennedy."

On her creative nature: "When I was in eighth grade, I won an award for a painting I did. It was kind of a big deal at the time - I got a statuette. But looking back, how major could it have been? It was a painting of a clown! Of course, that didn't take away from the excitement of it all. I think I've been chasing after that glory every since."
